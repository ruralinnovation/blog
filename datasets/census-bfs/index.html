Overview

The Business Formation Statistics (BFS) provides high-frequency data on new business applications based on IRS Employer Identification Number (EIN) applications (Form SS-4). BFS serves as a leading indicator of entrepreneurial activity and future business formation.

BFS includes two key series:

  • Business Applications (BA) - All applications likely to be a new business
  • High-Propensity Business Applications (HBA) - Applications with high likelihood of becoming employer businesses
NoteWhy BFS is Essential
  • High frequency - Monthly updates (state/national) for real-time tracking
  • Leading indicator - Predicts future business formation
  • Universal coverage - Administrative data, not survey
  • Long time series - Data back to 2004

Coverage

Geographic Coverage

  • County (annual, BA only)
  • State (monthly and annual, BA and HBA)
  • National (monthly and annual, BA and HBA)

Temporal Coverage

  • 2004-present
  • Monthly (state/national, ~2-week lag)
  • Annual (county, ~4-month lag)
  • Universe: IRS Form SS-4 applications

Key Variables

Business Applications (BA)

  • Total Applications - All filtered business applications
  • Applications by Sector - NAICS 2-digit industry classification
  • Applications by Organizational Type - Corporation, partnership, LLC, etc.

High-Propensity Business Applications (HBA)

  • High-Propensity Applications - Subset of BA with high likelihood of hiring employees
  • HBA by Sector - Industry classification
  • Not available at county level - Only state/national

Derived Metrics

  • Applications per 1,000 population - Normalized startup rate
  • Year-over-year growth rate - Business formation momentum

Data Structure

County Annual Format:

  • One row per county per year
  • GEOID: 5-digit FIPS code
  • BA series only (no HBA at county level)

State Monthly Format:

  • One row per state per month
  • Includes both BA and HBA series

Typical Variables:

geoid, year, business_applications,
applications_per_1k_population

Methodology

Data Source: IRS Form SS-4

BFS is derived from IRS applications for Employer Identification Numbers (EINs):

  • Required for hiring employees, opening bank accounts, filing taxes
  • Filed before business operations begin
  • Indicator of intent to start business

Filtering Algorithm

Census filters out non-business applications:

  • Personal use EINs
  • Trusts and estates
  • Government entities
  • Non-operating entities

Result: Business Applications (BA) series

High-Propensity Business Applications (HBA)

HBA subset includes applications with high likelihood of becoming employer firms:

  • Specific organizational structures (corporations, partnerships, LLCs)
  • Wage payment intentions
  • Certain industry classifications
ImportantConversion Rates

From NBER research (2024):

  • ~33% of BA are classified as HBA
  • ~34.5% of HBA become employer firms within 18 months
  • Net result: ~11-12% of all BA become employer businesses

Leading Indicator Properties

BFS is a leading indicator because:

  • Applications filed before business operations begin
  • Average lag: 18 months from application to business operation
  • Correlation with future BDS entry rates: 0.7-0.8

Research validation:

  • Bayard et al. (2024, NBER) confirms HBA predicts employer firm formation
  • HBA strongly correlated with subsequent job creation

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • High frequency (monthly)
  • Leading indicator
  • Universal coverage
  • Long time series (2004+)
  • Industry detail

Limitations

  • County: BA only (no HBA)
  • County: Annual only (no monthly)
  • Applications ≠ businesses (~11-12% become employers)
  • Organizational structure changes
  • No employment data
  • Disclosure suppression

Important Notes

County-Level Limitations

WarningCritical County Data Limitations

County BFS data has significant limitations:

  • Only Business Applications (BA) available, not HBA
  • Only ~33% of BA are high-propensity (HBA)
  • Only ~11-12% of BA actually become employer firms
  • 18-month lag on average from application to operation

Interpretation: County BA counts should be interpreted as intent to start business, not actual business formation. For actual employer businesses, use Census BDS.

Recent Methodology Changes (January 2026)

Census revised HBA methodology:

  • Internet sales now excluded from HBA/CBA series
  • Reduces HBA/CBA counts going forward
  • Creates time series break at January 2026
  • Documentation: BFS Methodology Update

Link to BDS

BFS is a leading indicator of Business Dynamics Statistics (BDS):

BFS (application) → 18 months → BDS (employer firm)

For historical analysis, use BDS (actual firms). For nowcasting, use BFS (applications).
