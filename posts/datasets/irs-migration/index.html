Overview

The IRS Statistics of Income (SOI) County-to-County Migration Data tracks the movement of people and income across U.S. counties based on year-to-year changes in tax return filing addresses. This dataset provides the most comprehensive view of where people are moving and how much income they bring with them.

Unlike Census migration data (which is survey-based), IRS migration data is based on administrative tax records, covering the vast majority of the U.S. population.

NoteWhy IRS Migration Data is Essential
  • Administrative data - Based on tax records, not surveys (high accuracy)
  • Income flows - Unique ability to track wealth movement with people
  • Origin-destination detail - Know exactly where people are moving from/to
  • High coverage - ~95% of population (tax filers + dependents)
  • Long time series - Data back to 1991

Coverage

Geographic Coverage

  • County-to-county migration flows
  • Origin-destination pairs

Temporal Coverage

  • 1991-present (annual)
  • CORI uses 2007-2022
  • Released ~2 years after tax year
  • Universe: All tax filers (~95% of U.S.)

Key Variables

Migration Flows

  • Returns (In-flow) - Number of tax returns that moved into the county
  • Returns (Out-flow) - Number of tax returns that moved out of the county
  • Exemptions (In-flow) - Number of people who moved in (returns + dependents)
  • Exemptions (Out-flow) - Number of people who moved out
  • Net Migration - In-flow minus out-flow

Income Flows

  • Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) In-flow - Total AGI of in-migrants (thousands of dollars)
  • AGI Out-flow - Total AGI of out-migrants
  • Net AGI Flow - In-flow minus out-flow

Derived Metrics (CORI Projects)

  • Net Migration Rate - Net migration as % of county population
  • Net AGI Migration Rate - Net AGI flow as % of county total AGI
  • Net Migration by Income Bracket - Who is moving? (high/middle/low income)

Data Structure

Raw Format (County-to-County Pairs)

  • One row per origin-destination county pair
  • Sparse matrix (only pairs with ≥10 returns reported)
  • FIPS codes for origin (y1) and destination (y2)

Example:

origin_fips, dest_fips, returns, exemptions, agi_thousands
06001, 06013, 245, 512, 18750
06001, 36061, 89, 156, 9820

Aggregated Format (Net Flows by County)

CORI aggregates to net flows:

geoid, year, net_migration_count, net_agi_flow,
share_of_net_migration, share_of_net_agi_migration

Methodology

Data Source: Tax Returns

IRS tracks address changes on Form 1040:

  • Year 1 (y1) address vs. Year 2 (y2) address
  • If addresses differ and in different counties → migration
  • Returns matched using Social Security Numbers

Coverage:

  • Tax filers - Required to file if income above threshold
  • Dependents - Counted as “exemptions”
  • Non-filers excluded - Very low-income, undocumented immigrants

Privacy Protection

To protect taxpayer privacy:

  • Flows < 10 returns suppressed - Small flows not reported
  • Aggregated only - No individual-level data
  • Same-county moves excluded - Only inter-county migration

AGI as Income Proxy

Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) measures:

Included:

  • Wages and salaries
  • Business income
  • Investment income
  • Retirement income

Excluded:

  • Social Security (reported separately)
  • Tax-exempt interest
  • Unrealized capital gains

AGI reflects “movable” income but has limitations.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Administrative data (high accuracy)
  • Income flows (unique capability)
  • Origin-destination detail
  • High coverage (~95%)
  • Long time series (1991+)

Limitations

  • Lagged release (18-24 months)
  • Excludes non-filers
  • Tax year vs. calendar year
  • Suppression (<10 returns)
  • Address changes only
  • AGI not deflated (nominal dollars)

Important Notes

Net Migration vs. Gross Flows

IRS data includes both:

  • Gross in-flow - Total people moving in
  • Gross out-flow - Total people moving out
  • Net migration - In-flow minus out-flow
TipExample

County gains 500 in-migrants, loses 400 out-migrants

  • Gross in-flow: 500
  • Net migration: +100

Many rural counties have high gross flows (people moving in and out) but small net migration (in and out roughly balance).

AGI Deflation (Important!)

IRS reports AGI in nominal dollars (not adjusted for inflation). For time series analysis:

# Deflate to real 2023 dollars using CPI-U-RS
real_agi_2023 = nominal_agi * (cpi_2023 / cpi_year)

CORI’s load_irs_soi.R script should include deflation (currently a TODO item).

Pandemic Migration Surge (2020-2021)

IRS data shows unprecedented migration during COVID-19:

  • 2020-2021 migration rates 30-50% higher than 2019
  • Large cities lost population to suburbs and smaller metros
  • Rural recreation counties (mountains, coasts) gained
  • Remote work enabled moves

Rural net migration turned positive for first time in decades in many counties.

Relationship to Census Data

IRS migration complements Census PEP Components of Change:

Dataset Strengths Limitations
IRS Origin-destination flows, income data, administrative 18-month lag, excludes non-filers
Census Components Faster release, all population, separate domestic/international No origin-destination, no income

Best practice: Use IRS for detailed flow analysis; use Census Components for timely total migration.

Related Datasets

  • Census PEP Components of Change - Faster migration counts (no income or flows)
  • Census Population Estimates - Population for per-capita rates
  • American Community Survey - Migration within past year (survey)
