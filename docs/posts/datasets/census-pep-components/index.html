Overview

The Census Population Estimates Program (PEP) Components of Change dataset decomposes annual population change into its underlying drivers:

  • Births - Natural increase component
  • Deaths - Natural decrease component
  • Migration - Domestic and international migration

This decomposition reveals why counties are growing or declining, enabling targeted policy responses. For example:

  • Is rural population decline due to out-migration or aging (more deaths than births)?
  • Are amenity counties growing from migration or natural increase?
  • How did pandemic mortality affect rural demographic trends?
NoteWhy Components Matter
  • Decomposed change - Understand why population is changing
  • Administrative data - Births and deaths from vital statistics (highly accurate)
  • Complete coverage - All counties, all years
  • Faster than IRS migration - Released ~12 months after reference year

Key Variables

Natural Change

  • Births - Total births in calendar year (from vital statistics)
  • Deaths - Total deaths in calendar year (from vital statistics)
  • Natural Change - Births minus deaths

Migration

  • Domestic Migration - Net migration from other U.S. counties
  • International Migration - Net migration from other countries
  • Net Migration - Domestic + international migration

Residual

  • Residual - Unexplained population change (census corrections, armed forces, group quarters)

Total Population Change

  • Population Change - Net change in population (natural change + net migration + residual)

Data Structure

County Annual Format:

  • One row per county per year
  • GEOID: 5-digit FIPS code
  • Separate columns for each component

Typical Variables:

Methodology

Component of Change Model

Population in year t is calculated as:

Pop(t) = Pop(t-1) + Births(t) - Deaths(t) + DomesticMig(t) + IntlMig(t) + Residual(t)

Data Sources by Component

Component Primary Data Source
Births National Vital Statistics System (NVSS) - State birth certificates
Deaths NVSS - State death certificates
Domestic Migration Residual estimation - IRS tax records, Medicare enrollment, ACS
International Migration DHS immigration data, ACS, emigration estimates
ImportantDomestic Migration is Estimated

Domestic migration is not directly measured. It’s estimated as a residual after accounting for births, deaths, and international migration.

Vintage Files

Census releases components in “vintage” batches:

  • Each vintage may revise earlier years based on new census benchmarks
  • CORI combines vintages to create consistent long time series
  • Accepting most recent vintage for each year (no retroactive revisions)

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Decomposed change (understand why)
  • Administrative data (births/deaths accurate)
  • Complete coverage
  • Faster than IRS migration
  • International migration included

Limitations

  • Domestic migration is residual (estimated)
  • No origin-destination flows
  • No migration by income
  • Group quarters challenge
  • Vintage revisions

Important Notes

Natural Increase vs. Natural Decrease

  • Natural Increase
  • Natural Decrease

Births > Deaths (population growing from natural change)

Deaths > Births (population declining from natural change)

As of 2023:

  • ~50% of rural counties have natural decrease
  • Driven by aging population (fewer women of childbearing age, more elderly)
  • Natural decrease is accelerating in rural America

Even if migration is positive, natural decrease can cause overall population decline.

Pandemic Mortality Impact (2020-2021)

Components data reveals COVID-19’s demographic impact:

  • Excess deaths: 2020-2021 death counts 15-20% above 2019 baseline
  • Rural counties hit harder in 2021 (delta wave)
  • Natural increase/decrease dramatically worsened

Domestic Migration Estimation Challenges

Domestic migration is the most uncertain component:

  • Estimated as residual after accounting for other components
  • Subject to large revisions when census data released
  • Can have negative migration even when county is attractive (if births + international migration undercounted)

Best practice: Use IRS migration data for detailed migration analysis (county-to-county flows, income).

Vintage Reconciliation Strategy

TipCORI’s Approach

CORI’s load_pep_components.R script combines 3 vintage files:

  1. 2000-2009 vintage (accepted as-is)
  2. 2010-2019 vintage (accepted as-is)
  3. 2020-2023 vintage (most recent)

No retroactive revisions - Accepting each vintage’s estimates for its period prevents time series discontinuities.

Additive Components

All components are additive:

  • Births, deaths, domestic migration, international migration, residual sum to total population change
  • Enables accounting for 100% of population change
  • Rates can be calculated using same denominator (population)

Related Datasets

  • Census Population Estimates - Total population (numerator for rates)
  • IRS County-to-County Migration - Detailed migration flows and income (complementary)
  • National Vital Statistics System (NVSS) - Direct access to birth/death microdata
